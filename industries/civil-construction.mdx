Heavy machinery and construction equipment need QR codes for daily inspections, maintenance tracking, and operator documentation. qrtub provides one QR code per asset connecting to every system operators and managers need.

## The Construction Challenge

Construction equipment operates in harsh environments with:
- Multiple operators across shifts and sites
- Daily pre-start inspection requirements
- Complex maintenance schedules
- Operator qualification tracking
- Equipment moving between job sites
- Compliance documentation needs

Each piece of equipment needs access to multiple systems—but covering machines with multiple QR code stickers is impractical and unprofessional.

## How qrtub Helps Construction Teams

### One Code Per Asset
Every excavator, loader, or crane gets a single QR code linking to all required systems:
- **SafetyCulture inspections** for daily pre-start checks
- **Maintenance logs** in your CMMS platform
- **Operator manuals** and safety documentation
- **Service history** and compliance records

Operators scan once and choose what they need. Clean presentation on equipment. No confusion about which code to scan.

### Print Before Deployment
Professional weatherproof QR codes require bulk printing and lead time. With qrtub:
- Order stickers before equipment arrives
- Apply codes during commissioning
- Connect to systems when convenient
- No dependency on perfect timing

**Example:** You order 200 equipment QR codes for a new project. Machinery arrives over 3 months from different suppliers. Apply codes as equipment is commissioned, connect to your systems on-site.

### Update Without Reprinting
Construction projects change. Systems evolve. QR codes need to adapt:
- Switch inspection apps without reprinting codes
- Add new maintenance platforms as needed
- Update documentation links when specs change
- Connect to different systems on different sites

Physical codes stay fixed. Digital destinations update instantly.

### Different Information for Different People

**Operators see:**
- Pre-start inspection checklist
- Daily check-in procedures
- Emergency contacts
- Operating instructions

**Maintenance teams see:**
- Service history and logs
- Maintenance schedules
- Parts documentation
- Technical specifications

**Site managers see:**
- Compliance documentation
- Certification status
- Utilization tracking
- Cost allocation

Same QR code. Relevant information for each role.

## Getting Started with Construction Equipment

### Step 1: Generate Links
Create Links for your equipment fleet. Bulk generation for professional printing. Use custom IDs matching your equipment numbering (e.g., `qrtub.com/exc001` for Excavator 001).

### Step 2: Order Professional Codes
Weatherproof vinyl stickers or durable metal plaques designed for equipment environments. Order in bulk for cost efficiency.

### Step 3: Deploy on Equipment
Apply QR codes during equipment commissioning or existing fleet upgrade. No rush—Links are ready whenever you are.

### Step 4: Connect to Systems
Set up Profile Pages linking to your inspection app, CMMS, documentation, and other required systems. Use URL Templates to auto-populate equipment IDs—configure the template once, every piece of equipment gets personalized URLs automatically.

### Step 5: Update as Needed
Add new systems, switch vendors, update procedures—all without touching physical equipment.

## Common Construction Scenarios

### Daily Pre-Start Inspections
Operator arrives, scans QR code, taps "Start Inspection," completes SafetyCulture checklist. Direct workflow. No searching for the right code.

### Service Due Notification
Maintenance scheduler identifies service due. Technician scans code, taps "Log Service," opens CMMS with equipment pre-selected. Efficient service documentation.

### New Operator Onboarding
New operator unfamiliar with equipment. Scans code, taps "Operating Manual," accesses full documentation and safety procedures. Self-service access to critical information.

### Equipment Transfer Between Sites
Equipment moves from Site A to Site B. Manager updates Profile Page destinations to Site B systems. Same code, new site configuration. No physical changes needed.
